I hadn't ever left the United States until I was 18. I had traveled all over the US many times but never to a foreign country. I did however, always have a facination with other cultures. My favorite classes in school were history. I loved learning about the Romans and the early British Empire and many other diffferent and interesting facits of world history. But I wouldn't come to realize my facination with the outside world until I experienced it myself. The first place I evere travelled to was Germany. I went with my family as part of a graduation gift from highschool. The second I landed and was experiencing new language and new sights and foods and feelings I was instantly in love with being abroad. However, for many reasons, the biggest being that I was 18 and didn't know what I wanted. I didn't take any action on my new love for being abroad. Outside of the two weeks I spent travelling in Germany and Austria.

Germany

I first went to Germany in 2012 as part of a highschool graduation gift. I went with family to visit many of the different cities such as Berlin, Frankfurt, Cologne, and Munich. Since we were only ther for about two weeks we weren't able to do much in every single city, however, I was able to enjoy every second of it. My favorite city was probably Munich, at the time I had never really experienced a large city on the style that you can find in Europe, or Asia, or even New York. I'm from Houston, and while it is the fourth biggest city in the US we have so much land in Texas that their is no reason to build up. We can easily get by, by building out. So I wasn't used to the sprawling city scape of Berlin. So at the time Munich was more of my style as it is a perfect mix between the city center you can find in many urban cities as well as the open roads and sidewalks of a suburban area.
